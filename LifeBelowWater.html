Life Below Water

Every day, 8 million pieces of plastic find their way in our oceans. Thoses 8 million pieces of plastic

in the oceans can be potentially harmful to the species that live amongst them. Over 1 million species of

animals that live in the oceans are affected by plastic debris each year, and the number is on the rise.

If we don't start to take action, every species including ours, will fall victims to water pollution in some way.

Although all marine species can be potentially affected by plastic debris, these 3 animals are the most affected.

1. Sea Turtles

Unfortunately, sea turtles mistake plastic debris for a viable food source. When they consume the plastic, it sometimes causes

blockages in their digestive system.

2. Sea Birds

Plastic pollution leads to the deaths of millions of marine bird species each year. When a marine bird dives into the ocean to

catch their food, they use their beak to skim the surface, picking up plastic along the way. Shockingly, an estimated 98%

of albatross studied, are found having ingested some kind of plastic debris.

3. Fish

Fish, along with pretty much any marine mammal that brings in water through its gills, are increasingly at risk to microscopic

plastic debris. A study performed at the University of Exeter UK suggested that microscopic marine debris could take up to six

times as long for the animal to rid themselves of in comparison to ingesting the debris orally.
