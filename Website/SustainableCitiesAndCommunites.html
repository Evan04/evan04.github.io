Sustainable Cities and Communities

Currently, there are approximately 7.53 billion people on Earth. If every single person were to be

throwing out their garbage in the proper bins, then there would be no animals getting affected by the

debris. Unfortunately, not everyone cares about where their garbage goes, so that leads to many severe

consequences.

1. Death of marine animals

Majority of the time, marine animals like sea turtles, birds, or fish, accidentally consume plastic debris, thinking it's a source of

food. And for most of the time, those animals who consumed the plastic debris don't live for much long.

2. Flooding

Littering can clog storm-water drains, causing floods. These floods can ruin households, and can cause even more debris to enter

the oceans

3. Contaminated Drinking Water

Plastic debris and other microscopic organisms from pieces of garbage found in our oceans, can one day cause our drinking water

to be contaminated. Majority of garbage found in the oceans carry many fatal bacteria which can be harmful if it ends up in

our drinking water.

How many people live in cities?

3.5 billion people, half of the world’s population, live in cities. By 2050, the urban population is expected to reach 6.5 billion.

Approximately how many people live in slums?

There are about 828 million people that live in slums, and the number is on the rise.

How many jobs depend on a healthy and stable environment?

Around 1.2 billion jobs depend on a healthy and stable environment.
